Map details

These maps show how the percentage of Black population in US counties from 1970 to 2020. The third map highlights the "racial transition," where a county had a Black population below the state median in 1970 but increased more than the state average change from 1970 to 2020. Counties going through this transition are marked in blue; others are in green. The areas with the highest percentage Black population are near the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico both in 1970 and 2020. Notably, some counties in Minnesota, North Dakota, Texas, and Nevada experienced racial transitions.
